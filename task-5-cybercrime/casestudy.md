# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India today.
It involves tricking users into authorizing fraudulent transactions or revealing sensitive banking details.
A typical UPI fraud begins when a victim receives a phone call, message, or link claiming to be from a bank,
delivery service, or customer support. The fraudster may create urgency, such as claiming a problem with the user’s account or offering a reward. 
The victim is then asked to click on a link, install an app, or approve a “collect request” on their UPI app. 
In some cases, the fraudster convinces the user to share their OTP or PIN, which should never be disclosed.
College students and young adults are often targeted because they frequently use digital payments and may act quickly without verifying details.
Once access is gained, the fraudster can transfer money instantly, often leaving little time to react.
The consequences include financial loss, stress, and potential misuse of personal data.
Recovery can be difficult if the fraud is not reported immediately.
This case highlights the importance of awareness, careful verification, 
and responsible digital behavior while using online payment systems.
